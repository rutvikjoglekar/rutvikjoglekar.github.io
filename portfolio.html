Data science and analytics portfolio

I have substantial experience with statistical programming (i.e., R, Python, SAS, and Stata) from my time as an MBA student at MIT, economic consultant at Bates White, and undergraduate economics student at UChicago.

While at MIT, my coursework either expanded or heavily leveraged my data analytics skills. For example, in Alphanomics (15.465), I wrote scripts that tapped into the AlphaVantage API to download various financial metrics (e.g., income statement) for a randomized set of publicly-traded stocks. I then calculated Piotroski f-scores and after backtesting, show that stocks with higher f-scores can outperform the market. Other data-heavy courses included Data, Models, and Decisions (15.060), the Analytics Edge (15.071) , and the Airline Industry (16.71) .

Bates White is an economic consulting firm that provides advanced economic analysis to F500 firms and government agencies in litigation. I worked on projects ranging from estimating a pharmaceutical firm’s financial liability in the opioid crisis to valuing cancer claims in a product liability dispute. I was also responsible for translating technical analyses into language understandable to a jury for expert reports that would be submitted to court. Toward the end of my time at Bates White, I was managing case teams.

As an undergrad at UChicago, I was hired as a data science intern at Nielsen and as an RA for the Consortium on School Research. In my favorite project, I analyzed data from Princeton’s Empirical Studies of Conflicts (ESOC) project to isolate factors that drove voter turnout in the December 2005 Iraqi elections. I found that aside from education, public infrastructure (e.g., stable public electricity, garbage collection) have significant effects on democratic participation.

Product portfolio

I've worked at a range of firms as a product manager, from small startups to large Fortune 500 firms.

Bonfire Analytics is a pre-seed startup optimizing customer acquisition for healthcare tech B2B firms using ML-based lead scoring. I was tasked with expanding Bonfire's core 'Geographic Insights' product by adding a feature that identified all employer-sponsored health insurance plans offered in the US. I relied on data released by payers as part of the Federal government's 'Transparency in Coverage' legislation.

I interned at Best Buy as a product manager on the Total Tech team (BBY's membership platform) during Summer 2022. I worked on two main projects: (i) I led a cross-functional effort with UX, engineering, and data science to personalize messaging for Total Tech members on the home page and membership dashboard and (ii) worked closely with UX to draft a 4 question Totaltech onboarding survey for when a customer joins the program. The survey launched during Fall 2022.

LogRocket is a series-C funded startup with a front-end website monitoring tool to fix bugs. I interned at LogRocket in product operations during Fall 2021. I analyzed datasets containing network errors to roadmap Proactive Insights, a feature that automatically identifies high-impact bugs on customer’s websites. I also collaborated with the Customer Success team to collect feedback from Proactive Insights trial customers and revised the roadmap to prioritize errors affecting sales funnels.

HarrisX is a market research startup that joins survey data with mobile telemetry data to capture American consumer attitudes. I worked at HarrisX in a variety of roles, including as a product manager, from 2018-2019. Two of my projects included (i) designing and building a Python-based tool to auto-track competitors' device offerings and prices for a mobile provider and (ii) launching an internal tool to auto-compile commonly-used metrics from firm's suite of data products.
