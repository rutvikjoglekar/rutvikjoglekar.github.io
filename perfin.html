Personal finance: a guide on how to DIY

Navigating personal finance is really, really hard! No one teaches us about this stuff as we grow up.

So I built this page out with a lot of information that I've learned since I graduated college, and these are the exact things I would do if I were to step back into the shoes of 22-year-old me.

A lot of this can seem convoluted and confusing on first glance, but I promise it isn't!

Banking

You shouldn't be paying anything to bank! In fact, the bank should be paying you because they have the privilege of handling your cash. Explore the modules below to see how you can get the most juice from your bank.

Small changes go a long way.
If you did any of the following in 2023: Traditional bank DIYFi rec. solution
Held an emergency fund of $10K Earned $47 in interest Earned $435 in interest
Withdrew cash from an ATM unaffiliated with your bank Paid $4 per trans. Paid $0 per trans.
Withdrew cash abroad Paid 3% foreign transaction fee + ATM fees Paid $0 per trans.

Note: Savings account interest rate and ATM fee estimates from Bankrate.

Investing

Investing shouldn't be hard! Explore the modules below to replicate a portfolio exactly like a financial manager or robo-advisor (e.g., Wealthfront or Betterment) would without paying a single cent in advisory fees.

If you started investing $10K every year since 2020, your advisory fees* would amount to:
Year Financial advisor (FA) Robo-advisor Do it yourself (DIY)
2024 $1,891 $473 $0
2030 $9,410 $2,352 $0
2040 $42,560 $10,640 $0

* Assumes 1% fee for FA, 0.25% fee for robo-advisor, and 7% future market returns.
